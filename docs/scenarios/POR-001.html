POR-001 · Terminal operating system ransomware halting container movement

An attacker delivers ransomware into a container terminal's IT environment, encrypts the Terminal Operating System (TOS) database, and indirectly halts crane and gate operations because cargo movement is gated on TOS lookups. The pattern is consistent with multiple disclosed incidents at major international ports (Maersk/NotPetya 2017, Port of Nagoya 2023) and the 2020 Toll Group incident in Australia. Even where OT systems are not directly compromised, dependency on IT-side scheduling and gate systems results in operational paralysis.

Priors (Beta-PERT)

Annual frequency: min 0.01 · mode 0.06 · max 0.25

Ports are high-value ransomware targets. AU has fewer than 10 SOCI port operators but global incident rate at major terminals indicates a per- operator annual mode in the 5-8% range. Recent AU history (Toll 2020, DP World Australia 2023) supports the upper bound.
