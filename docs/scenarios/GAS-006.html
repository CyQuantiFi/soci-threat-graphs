GAS-006 · Cathodic-protection IoT tampering accelerating pipeline corrosion

An attacker compromises the cellular-connected cathodic-protection (CP) monitoring devices on a buried pipeline and silently degrades the protection currents. The tampering does not cause an immediate event; instead, it accelerates corrosion over months, dramatically shortening the asset's remaining useful life and creating eventual leak or rupture risk. Pattern emerges from public IoT-device security research and Dragos and CISA reports on long-dwell ICS intrusions.
