WAT-001 · SCADA compromise via engineering workstation pivot

An attacker compromises an internet-exposed engineering workstation used by control engineers, pivots into the SCADA HMI over a dual-homed jump host, and issues unauthorised commands to a PLC controlling chemical dosing at a water treatment plant. The pattern is documented across multiple public incidents including the 2021 Oldsmar (Florida) water-plant intrusion and is consistent with CISA advisory AA21-287A on ongoing threats to US water utilities. The corresponding pattern is credible for AU operators given shared vendor stacks and similar operational architectures.

Suggested controls

  • PR.AC-5 — Network segmentation between IT and OT (NIST-CSF)
  • E8-PATCH-INTERNET-FACING — Patch internet-facing services within 48 hours (Essential-Eight)
  • AESCSF-AC-04 — Privileged access management on OT jump hosts (AESCSF)
  • CIRMP-CYBER-7 — Documented OT change-management process with two-person rule (SOCI-CIRMP)
