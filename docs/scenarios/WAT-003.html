WAT-003 · Internet-exposed HMI directly accessed by opportunistic adversary

A small water utility's HMI or VNC server is reachable from the public internet, either by design (unmanaged remote-support) or by misconfiguration. An opportunistic attacker discovers the asset through internet scanning (Shodan, Censys), authenticates with default or weak credentials, and issues setpoint changes that affect treatment dosing or pump scheduling. Pattern is consistent with the Oldsmar 2021 incident and the broader CISA alerts AA22-103A and AA22-083A on water-sector exposures.

Suggested controls

  • E8-PATCH-INTERNET-FACING — Patch internet-facing services within 48 hours (Essential-Eight)
  • PR.AC-5 — Network segmentation, no direct internet exposure of OT (NIST-CSF)
  • CIRMP-CYBER-3 — Inventory of internet-reachable services and quarterly external attack-surface review (SOCI-CIRMP)
  • AESCSF-AC-04 — Privileged access management on remote-access pathways (AESCSF)
